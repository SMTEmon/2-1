### 1. The Core Problem: When $Ax = b$ Has No Solution

Usually, we solve $Ax = b$ when $b$ lies in the column space $C(A)$. However, in real-world data, $b$ often lies outside $C(A)$, meaning there is no solution.

- **The Goal**: Find the **closest possible solution** $\hat{x}$ such that $A\hat{x} = p$, where $p$ is the **projection** of $b$ onto the column space.
- **The Error Vector ($e$)**: The distance between the actual data and the prediction is $e = b - p$.
- **Orthogonality Principle**: For $p$ to be the closest point to $b$, the error vector $e$ must be **perpendicular (orthogonal)** to the subspace. This means $A^T e = 0$, which leads to the **Normal Equation**: $$A^T A \hat{x} = A^T b$$.

### 2. Projection Matrix ($P$)

The projection matrix $P$ is what you multiply by $b$ to "land" on the projection $p$ ($p = Pb$).

- **Projection onto a line ($a$)**: $P = \frac{aa^T}{a^T a}$.
- **Projection onto a subspace ($A$)**: $P = A(A^T A)^{-1} A^T$.
- **Key Properties of $P$**:
    - **Symmetric**: $P^T = P$.
    - **Idempotent**: $P^2 = P$ (projecting twice doesn't change the result).
    - **Rank**: For a projection onto a line, the rank is always 1.

### 3. Least Square Regression (LSR)

LSR is the application of projection to find the "best-fitting line" through data points.

- **Minimizing Error**: The goal is to minimize the **Sum of Squared Errors** ($MSE$).
- **Best-Fit Line**: For a model $y = mx + c$, you set up the matrix $A$ where the first column is the $x$ values and the second is a column of ones (to account for the intercept $c$).

#### **2.1 Transfer Market***

You need to develop a model $y = \beta_0 + \beta_1x_1 + \beta_2x_2$ to estimate player fees.

- **Coefficient Matrix ($A$)**: Each row represents a player. The first column is all **1s** (for $\beta_0$), the second is $x_1$ (G/A), and the third is $x_2$ (Age). $$A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 18 & 23 \ 1 & 6 & 25 \ 1 & 14 & 24 \ 1 & 24 & 26 \end{bmatrix}, \quad b = \begin{bmatrix} 95 \ 60 \ 35 \ 120 \end{bmatrix}$$
- **Step i)**: Solve the **Normal Equation** $A^T A \hat{x} = A^T b$ to find the vector of coefficients $\hat{x} = [\beta_0, \beta_1, \beta_2]^T$.
- **Step ii)**: Calculate the error vector $e = b - A\hat{x}$ and the **Mean Squared Error** ($MSE = \frac{||e||^2}{n}$).
- **Step iii)**: Predict prices for the scouted players (Saka, Pedri, etc.) using your model and check if they are $\leq 80$ million.
