### 1. Orthogonality of Vectors

- **Fundamental Definition**: Two vectors $v$ and $w$ are orthogonal if they are **perpendicular**, which is mathematically proven if their **dot product is zero** ($v \cdot w = 0$ or $v^T w = 0$).
- **The Zero Vector**: Every vector is orthogonal to the zero vector.
- **Pythagorean Theorem**: For two orthogonal vectors, the square of the length of their sum equals the sum of the squares of their lengths: $||x||^2 + ||y||^2 = ||x + y||^2$. This is verified by the expansion $(x+y)^T(x+y) = x^Tx + y^Ty + 2x^Ty$, where $2x^Ty$ must be zero.

### 2. Orthogonality of Subspaces

- **Definition**: Two subspaces $V$ and $W$ in a vector space are orthogonal if **every vector** in $V$ is orthogonal to **every vector** in $W$ ($v^T w = 0$ for all $v \in V$ and $w \in W$).
- **Essential Conditions**:
    - **Intersection**: If two subspaces intersect at any vector other than the zero vector, they **cannot** be orthogonal.
    - **Dimension Rule**: Orthogonality is possible only when the sum of the dimensions of the two subspaces is less than or equal to the dimension of the entire vector space ($\dim(V) + \dim(W) \leq \dim(\text{vectorspace})$).
- **Practical Examples**:
    - **Floor and Wall**: While they seem perpendicular, they are **not** orthogonal subspaces because they share a line of non-zero vectors (the base of the wall), and vectors within the floor are not orthogonal to every vector in the wall.
    - **Floor and Corner**: The floor and the vertical line forming a corner are orthogonal because the line only meets the floor at the zero vector (origin) and is perpendicular to every vector in that plane.

### 3. The "Big Picture": Orthogonal Complements

The most critical part of this chapter is the relationship between the **four fundamental subspaces** of a matrix $A$.

- **Orthogonal Complements**: These are subspaces that are not just orthogonal but "complement" each other to fill the entire space.
    1. **Row Space and Nullspace**: The row space $C(A^T)$ is the orthogonal complement of the nullspace $N(A)$ in $R^n$ ($C(A^T) \perp N(A)$).
    2. **Column Space and Left Nullspace**: The column space $C(A)$ is the orthogonal complement of the left nullspace $N(A^T)$ in $R^m$ ($C(A) \perp N(A^T)$).
- **Logic of $Ax = 0$**: The equation $Ax=0$ inherently means that the vector $x$ (in the nullspace) has a dot product of zero with every row of $A$. Therefore, every vector in the nullspace is perpendicular to every row (and every linear combination of rows) in the row space.
- **Dimensions**:
    - In $R^n$: $\dim(C(A^T)) = r$ and $\dim(N(A)) = n - r$. Their sum is $r + (n-r) = n$.
    - In $R^m$: $\dim(C(A)) = r$ and $\dim(N(A^T)) = m - r$. Their sum is $r + (m-r) = m$.
