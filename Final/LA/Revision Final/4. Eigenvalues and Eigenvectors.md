### 1. Conceptual Foundation

- **Eigenvectors ($v$)**: These are special vectors that, when multiplied by a matrix $A$, stay in their original **span** (they only get stretched or squeezed along the same line).
- **Eigenvalues ($\lambda$)**: The scaling factor by which the eigenvector is stretched or squeezed.
- **The Eigen-Equation**: This relationship is expressed as **$Av = \lambda v$**.
- **Geometric Insight**: For most vectors, the transformation $Ax$ results in a new direction. An eigenvector is "immune" to this rotation.

### 2. How to Solve for $v$ and $\lambda$

To find these values, we solve the **characteristic equation**, which assumes the matrix $(A - \lambda I)$ must be singular (squeezing space into a lower dimension):

1. **Find $\lambda$**: Solve the polynomial equation **$\det(A - \lambda I) = 0$**.
2. **Find $v$**: For each $\lambda$, solve the system **$(A - \lambda I)v = 0$** to find the nullspace.

### 3. Key Properties for Verification

- **Trace**: The sum of the eigenvalues equals the sum of the diagonal entries of the matrix ($\sum \lambda_i = \text{Trace}(A)$).
- **Determinant**: The product of the eigenvalues equals the determinant of the matrix ($\prod \lambda_i = \det(A)$).
- **Matrix Type Characteristics**:
    - **Projection Matrix**: Eigenvalues are always **0 and 1**.
    - **Reflection Matrix**: Eigenvalues are always **1 and -1**.
    - **Rotation Matrix**: Eigenvalues appear as **complex conjugate pairs**.

### 4. Assignment Solution (Section 4.2)*

Find the eigenvalues and eigenvectors of $A = \begin{bmatrix} -2 & -4 & 2 \ -2 & 1 & 2 \ 4 & 2 & 5 \end{bmatrix}$.

**Step 1: Solve $\det(A - \lambda I) = 0$** The characteristic equation is calculated as: $$\lambda^3 - 4\lambda^2 - 27\lambda + 90 = 0$$ By solving this cubic equation, we obtain the **eigenvalues**: $$\mathbf{\lambda_1 = -5, \quad \lambda_2 = 6, \quad \lambda_3 = 3}$$

**Step 2: Solve $(A - \lambda I)v = 0$ for each $\lambda$**

- **For $\lambda_1 = -5$**: Solve $\begin{bmatrix} 3 & -4 & 2 \ -2 & 6 & 2 \ 4 & 2 & -10 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} a \ b \ c \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \ 0 \ 0 \end{bmatrix}$. Setting $a=1$ leads to $b=1$ and $c=0.5$. **$v_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 0.5 \end{bmatrix}^T$** (or any scalar multiple like $^T$).
- **For $\lambda_2 = 6$**: Solve $\begin{bmatrix} -8 & -4 & 2 \ -2 & -5 & 2 \ 4 & 2 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} a \ b \ c \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \ 0 \ 0 \end{bmatrix}$. Setting $a=1$ leads to $b=-1$ and $c=2$. **$v_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & 2 \end{bmatrix}^T$**.
- **For $\lambda_3 = 3$**: Solve $\begin{bmatrix} -5 & -4 & 2 \ -2 & -2 & 2 \ 4 & 2 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} a \ b \ c \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \ 0 \ 0 \end{bmatrix}$. Setting $a=1$ leads to $b=-0.25$ and $c=-2$. **$v_3 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -0.25 & -2 \end{bmatrix}^T$** (or $[4, -1, -8]^T$).

**Verification**:

- Sum: $-5 + 6 + 3 = 4$. Trace: $-2 + 1 + 5 = 4$. (Matches).
- Product: $(-5)(6)(3) = -90$. Determinant: $-90$. (Matches).
