### 1. The Fundamental Formula: $A = U\Sigma V^T$

SVD factorizes a matrix $A$ into three specific components:

- **$U$**: An $m \times m$ orthogonal matrix. Its columns are the **orthonormal eigenvectors of $AA^T$**.
- **$\Sigma$**: An $m \times n$ diagonal matrix containing the **singular values ($\sigma$)** in descending order ($\sigma_1 \geq \sigma_2 \geq \dots \geq 0$).
- **$V^T$**: The transpose of an $n \times n$ orthogonal matrix $V$. The columns of $V$ are the **orthonormal eigenvectors of $A^T A$**.

### 2. How to Solve for SVD (Step-by-Step)

To decompose a matrix as required in **Question 7.1** of your assignment, follow these mathematical steps:

1. **Find $V$ and $\Sigma$ using $A^T A$**:
    - Compute the symmetric matrix $A^T A$.
    - Find its eigenvalues ($\lambda_1, \lambda_2, \dots$) and orthonormal eigenvectors ($v_1, v_2, \dots$).
    - The **singular values** are the square roots of these eigenvalues: **$\sigma_i = \sqrt{\lambda_i}$**.
2. **Find $U$**:
    - You can find $U$ by computing the orthonormal eigenvectors of $AA^T$.
    - Alternatively, use the formula **$u_i = \frac{Av_i}{\sigma_i}$** to find each column of $U$ directly from your results in Step 1.
3. **Construct the Matrices**:
    - Place $\sigma_i$ on the diagonal of $\Sigma$.
    - Place $u_i$ as columns in $U$ and $v_i$ as columns in $V$.

### 3. Geometric Intuition: Rotate-Stretch-Rotate

SVD reveals that any linear transformation can be broken down into three geometric stages:

1. **Rotation ($V^T$)**: The input vector is rotated into a new coordinate system.
2. **Stretching ($\Sigma$)**: The vector is stretched or squeezed along the axes by the singular values.
3. **Rotation ($U$)**: The stretched vector is rotated a final time into its output position.

### 4. Real-World Usefulness

- **Image Compression**: In large data sets like images, the first few singular values ($\sigma_1, \sigma_2, \dots$) carry the most "weight". By ignoring the smaller, insignificant values, you can significantly reduce file size while preserving essential details.
- **Noise Cancellation**: SVD is used in signal processing to separate the "signal" (large singular values) from the "noise" (tiny singular values).

**Note for your Exam**: Remember that for a **symmetric matrix**, SVD is essentially the same as the Spectral Theorem ($A = Q\Lambda Q^T$), but SVD is the generalized version that works when symmetry or squareness is missing.
