# Lecture 8: Solving $Ax = b$

## 1. Overview
In previous lectures, we looked at the Null Space ($Ax=0$). In this lecture, we look at the complete solution to the system $Ax=b$. We determine:
1.  **Solvability:** Under what conditions does a solution exist?
2.  **Structure:** How to find the complete solution ($x = x_p + x_n$).
3.  **Rank:** How the rank $r$ relates to the number of solutions.

---

## 2. Solvability Conditions

When does $Ax=b$ have a solution?
Consider the example matrix $A$:
$$
A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 2 & 2 \\ 2 & 4 & 6 & 8 \\ 3 & 6 & 8 & 10 \end{bmatrix}
$$
Note that the third row is the sum of the first two rows ($R_3 = R_1 + R_2$).

### The Augmented Matrix
To solve $Ax=b$, we form the **Augmented Matrix** by tacking the vector $b$ onto the end of $A$ as an extra column.
$$
[A \ | \ b] = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 2 & 2 & | & b_1 \\ 2 & 4 & 6 & 8 & | & b_2 \\ 3 & 6 & 8 & 10 & | & b_3 \end{bmatrix}
$$

### Elimination on the Augmented Matrix
We perform elimination to reach Row Echelon Form. Importantly, we apply the same row operations to $b$ that we apply to $A$.

1.  Subtract $2 \times R_1$ from $R_2$.
2.  Subtract $3 \times R_1$ from $R_3$.

$$
\rightarrow \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 2 & 2 & | & b_1 \\ 0 & 0 & 2 & 4 & | & b_2 - 2b_1 \\ 0 & 0 & 2 & 4 & | & b_3 - 3b_1 \end{bmatrix}
$$

3.  Subtract $1 \times R_2$ (new) from $R_3$.

$$
\rightarrow \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 2 & 2 & | & b_1 \\ 0 & 0 & 2 & 4 & | & b_2 - 2b_1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & | & b_3 - b_2 - b_1 \end{bmatrix}
$$

> [!important] Solvability Condition
> The last row represents the equation:
> $$0x_1 + 0x_2 + 0x_3 + 0x_4 = b_3 - b_2 - b_1$$
> For a solution to exist, we **must** have $0 = 0$. Therefore, the condition for solvability is:
> $$b_3 - b_2 - b_1 = 0 \quad \text{or} \quad b_3 = b_1 + b_2$$

### General Solvability Rules
$Ax=b$ is solvable exactly when:
1.  **Algebraic:** If rows of $A$ calculate to the zero row, the corresponding components of $b$ must calculate to zero.
2.  **Geometric:** $b$ must be in the **Column Space** of $A$ ($b \in C(A)$).
    *   Since $R_3 = R_1 + R_2$ in matrix A, specific combinations of columns exist on the LHS. The same combination must exist for $b$ to be a valid output.

## 3. The Complete Solution ($x = x_p + x_n$)

If the solvability conditions are met, how do we find the solution? 
Let's choose specific $b$ values that satisfy the condition (e.g., $b = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 5 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix}$).

The complete solution is composed of two parts:
$$x_{complete} = x_{particular} + x_{nullspace}$$

### Step 1: Find a Particular Solution ($x_p$)
We find **one** solution to $Ax=b$.
**Method:** Set all **free variables** to zero and solve for the **pivot variables**.

*   **Free variables:** $x_2, x_4$ (columns with no pivots). Set $x_2=0, x_4=0$.
*   **Pivot variables:** $x_1, x_3$.

Substituting into our eliminated system:
1.  $2x_3 + 4(0) = 3 \implies 2x_3 = 3 \implies x_3 = 3/2$
2.  $x_1 + 2(0) + 2(3/2) + 2(0) = 1 \implies x_1 + 3 = 1 \implies x_1 = -2$

$$
x_p = \begin{bmatrix} -2 \\ 0 \\ 3/2 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}
$$

### Step 2: Find the Null Space Solution ($x_n$)
This is the general solution to $Ax=0$ (covered in Lecture 7). This accounts for the free variables being anything, not just zero.
We calculate the special solutions for the free variables ($x_2=1, x_4=0$) and ($x_2=0, x_4=1$).

$$
x_n = c_1 \begin{bmatrix} -2 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} + c_2 \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 0 \\ -2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}
$$

### Step 3: Combine them
The logic is based on linearity:
*   $A x_p = b$
*   $A x_n = 0$
*   $A (x_p + x_n) = A x_p + A x_n = b + 0 = b$

**Final Answer:**
$$
x = \begin{bmatrix} -2 \\ 0 \\ 3/2 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} + c_1 \begin{bmatrix} -2 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} + c_2 \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 0 \\ -2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}
$$

### Geometric Interpretation
*   $x_n$ is a **subspace** (a plane passing through the origin).
*   $x_p$ shifts that subspace away from the origin.
*   The solution set is a **flat** (like a line or plane) that does **not** pass through the origin.

> [!TIP] Visual
> Think of $x_n$ as a plane through $(0,0,0)$. Think of $x_p$ as a vector pointing to a spot on that plane. The complete solution is the entire plane shifted by the vector $x_p$.

## 4. Rank and Solutions Summary

The size of the matrix ($m$ by $n$) and its rank ($r$) tell us everything about the number of solutions.

Given an $m \times n$ matrix of rank $r$:
*   $r \le m$ and $r \le n$.

There are four main cases:

### Case 1: Full Column Rank ($r = n$)
*   Every column has a pivot.
*   **No free variables.**
*   The Null Space $N(A) = \{0\}$ (only the zero vector).
*   **Solution:** Either **0 or 1 solution**.
    *   Unique solution if $b$ exists (if solvable).
    *   No solution if $b$ is not in $C(A)$.
*   *Shape:* Tall and thin matrix ($m \ge n$).

### Case 2: Full Row Rank ($r = m$)
*   Every row has a pivot.
*   Elimination never produces a row of zeros.
*   **Solvability:** Solvable for **every** $b$.
*   There are $n-m$ free variables.
*   **Solution:** $\infty$ solutions for every $b$.
*   *Shape:* Short and wide matrix ($m \le n$).

### Case 3: Full Rank ($r = m = n$)
*   Square matrix.
*   Invertible.
*   Row Reduced Echelon Form $R = I$ (Identity).
*   **Solution:** **1 unique solution** for every $b$.

### Case 4: Deficient Rank ($r < m$ and $r < n$)
*   Not all rows have pivots (zero rows appear).
*   Not all columns have pivots (free variables exist).
*   **Solution:** Either **0 or $\infty$ solutions**.
    *   0 if solvability condition fails.
    *   $\infty$ if solvability condition is met.

### Summary Table

| Rank | Dimensions | Description | # Solutions |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **$r = m = n$** | Square | Invertible | **1** (Unique) |
| **$r = m < n$** | Short & Wide | Full Row Rank | **$\infty$** |
| **$r = n < m$** | Tall & Thin | Full Col Rank | **0 or 1** |
| **$r < m, r < n$** | Any | Deficient | **0 or $\infty$** |

---

## 5. Key Vocabulary
*   **Augmented Matrix:** Matrix $[A \ b]$ used during elimination.
*   **Particular Solution ($x_p$):** A single vector solving $Ax=b$, usually found by setting free variables to zero.
*   **Homogeneous Solution ($x_n$):** The complete set of vectors solving $Ax=0$ (the Null Space).
*   **Full Column Rank:** $r=n$. Columns are independent.
*   **Full Row Rank:** $r=m$. Rows are independent.
